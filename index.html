How do I use PitchSense?

Take or upload a photo of your recruit's Motivations & Grades screen in CFB26. PitchSense will read the green and red checkmarks, ask for your recruit's dealbreaker, and tell you exactly what recruiting action to take.

Why isn't it reading my screenshot correctly?

The image processor isn't perfect. After each scan, review the motivation toggles and grade dropdowns and correct anything that looks off before hitting 'What's My Move?'

How do I fix wrong grades or checkmarks?

Tap any motivation toggle to switch between green, red, and unknown. Tap any grade dropdown to select the correct grade. Changes are reflected instantly in the recommendation.

What is a dealbreaker and where do I find it?

A dealbreaker is one of your recruit's known motivations. You can find it on the recruit's profile screen in CFB26 in the bottom right corner window.

What does Send the House mean?

Send the House means there isn't enough information yet to identify the recruit's pitch. Keep recruiting and gathering information before committing to a specific pitch.

Why does it say Send the House even when I think I know the pitch?

Your grades may not be high enough. Each letter grade has a point value (F=1 through A+=13). If the total score across your recruit's three green motivations is under 20, Send the House is the safer play regardless of whether the pitch is identified.

What's the difference between Hard Sell and Double Soft Sell?

A Hard Sell means PitchSense has identified exactly one matching pitch and your grades are strong enough to commit. A Double Soft Sell means it's been narrowed to two possible pitches and you'll find out which one is correct the following week. For maximum impact, add an additional 10 hours by selecting DM the Player since Double Soft Sell only uses 40 recruiting hours.

Does this work for CFB 25?

No. PitchSense is built specifically for CFB26's recruiting system.

Why does it say servers are busy?

Occasionally high demand causes temporary delays. Wait a few seconds and try again.
